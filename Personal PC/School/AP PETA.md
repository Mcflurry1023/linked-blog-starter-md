	Capital Punishment in the Philippines
Capital punishment is the state-sanctioned execution of a citizen who has committed a severe offense. Rational choice theory models how an individual or group would act and react in uncertain situations for their own self interest. In its simplest form, it is the weighing of the costs and benefits of an action. In theory, this is to be used as a deterrent for any criminal, but with the abolishment of capital punishment in the Philippines under R.A. No. 9346 in 2006, the script has been flipped. It is now up to the government to see if the justice system of the Philippines should resume weighing the lives of thousands of serious offenders. The cost of letting hundreds of murderers live or hundreds of wrongfully convicted low-income civilians die. The cost of removing a sense of final justice to those affected or tell the families of the judged by the corrupt powers that be that their family member is dead. The cost of wide criticism from the entire world or the loud criticism of the victims affected. The legislators, in such a situation like this, will be predictable in their actions, modeled by probable utility and probability
